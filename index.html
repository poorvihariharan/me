Here's a little more about who I am beyond the resume.

My story

I graduated from UNC Chapel Hill in December 2024 with a degree in Communications (New Media) and a Minor in Entrepreneurship. My whole academic path was driven by my desire to connect with people, build experiences, and broaden my perspectives.

That drive has followed me through every role I've held, from designing engagement programs at IBM Research as an intern, to working in UX at Sapta Inc, to now building out North America's entry level hiring experience at IBM as Digital Engagement Lead.

How I work

I love fun, and I love connecting with people. Before I design anything, I want to talk to people, I want to understand the people I am designing for. What their perspectives are, their friction points, their unspoken needs.

I then use that as the framework for everything I go on to do: content strategy, prototype development, engagement frameworks, and more. I also focus on building things that can work on their own, making my projects scalable, clear, and repeatable.

Beyond the work

Since I was a baby, or, as I like to say, since before I was born, I have been in the world of meditation, breathwork, and mindfulness. This has been a large part of my life... to the point that almost everyone I know, I know through theArt of Living Foundation.

My life has been dedicated to moving the vision of Gurudev Sri Sri Ravi Shankar forward, which is to see a smile on every individual's face. Since 2016, I've been a certified breathwork and meditation instructor with the Art of Living Foundation. Teaching has shaped how I think about communication, presence, and meeting people where they are. It's one of the most meaningful things I do.

I speak three languages, Tamil, Hindi, and English. Which has greatly influenced how I think about inclusive, cross cultural experience design.

When I'm not working, I am most of the time with my family and on some new adventure, or I am meditating!
